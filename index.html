No DMs, Please 🙅‍♂️

Private messages at work slow us down. Here's why we ask everyone to use public channels instead.

🚪 1. Public Channels Create Shared Context

Public conversations let others see what’s happening, reducing repeated questions and improving visibility for the team.

🔍 2. Your Question Might Help Others

Most questions aren’t unique. Asking publicly helps more than just you—it builds searchable team knowledge.

🧠 3. Don’t Rely on One Brain

In a DM, only one person can help. In a channel, everyone can. It’s faster and more robust.

🕵️‍♀️ 4. DMs Are Opaque

There’s no history others can follow. Public channels let people learn from past discussions and decisions.

🛠️ 5. It Makes Onboarding Easier

New hires can scroll through channels and learn by observation. In DMs, that context is lost.

💬 When Are DMs Okay?

  • Personal or sensitive topics
  • Quick logistics ("ready for the call?")
  • 1:1 feedback or private notes
“Default to public. Ask in channels. Collaborate out loud.”
